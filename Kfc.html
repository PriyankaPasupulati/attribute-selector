Kfc

KFC, or Kentucky Fried Chicken, is a popular global fast-food chain known for its fried chicken. Founded by Colonel Harland Sanders in 1952, KFC has grown into one of the largest fast-food chains in the world, with thousands of locations in over 145 countries. KFC's signature dish is its fried chicken, which is typically made with a secret blend of 11 herbs and spices. The chicken pieces are breaded and fried until crispy and golden brown. In addition to its original recipe chicken, KFC offers various other chicken products such as chicken tenders, chicken sandwiches, and chicken wings. Aside from chicken, KFC also offers a variety of side dishes like mashed potatoes, coleslaw, macaroni and cheese, and biscuits, as well as desserts and beverages. KFC's distinctive flavor and convenient locations have made it a favorite choice for many people around the world when craving fried chicken or a quick meal on the go.
