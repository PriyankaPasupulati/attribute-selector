Pani-Puri

Ah, pani puri! It's a beloved Indian street food, also known as golgappa in some regions. Pani puri consists of small, hollow, crispy puris (round, deep-fried bread) filled with a mixture of flavored water, tamarind chutney, chaat masala, potato, chickpeas, and sometimes sprouts or other fillings, depending on regional variations. To enjoy pani puri, you typically make a small hole in the puri, fill it with the filling mixture, and then dip it into a bowl of tangy, spicy, and flavorful water called "pani" before popping the whole thing into your mouth. It's an explosion of flavors and textures and is immensely popular across India. Pani puri is not just a snack; it's an experience, often enjoyed with friends and family at roadside stalls or in food courts. Have you had the chance to try it?
